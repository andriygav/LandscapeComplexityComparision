## On the Landscape Complexity Measure for Fully Connected ReLU Networks under Squared Loss

[**Andrey Grabovoy**](https://intsystems.github.io/people/grabovoy_av/index.html)<br>
Institute of Control Sciences of the Russian Academy of Sciences
</div>

<br>

> **Abstract:** *The loss landscape and classical uniform-complexity measures capture different facets of neural models, yet their roles in regression deserve a clear side-by-side account.
> We study scalar regression with fully connected ReLU networks under the squared loss and compare empirical Rademacher complexity with a landscape complexity measure built from the spectral dispersion of per-example Hessians around the sample average.
> For bias-free, norm-bounded fully connected networks, we adapt spectral Hessian bounds from prior fully connected work to mean squared error, where the outer loss Hessian in the output has unit spectral norm, and use the empirical-risk increment only as a route to motivate and interpret this Hessian-dispersion measure.
> We describe how this landscape complexity measure scales with depth, width, and uniform bounds, summarize its growth relative to Rademacher complexity for linear and two-layer predictors in one table, and present numerical comparisons of the two notions on controlled data.*
